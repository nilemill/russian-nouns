The Case System in Russian: Ох, уж эти падежи!

The case system in Russian does two things. First, it marks the grammatical functions of nouns which are indicated by word order in English, that is, the subject, object and indirect object of the sentence. Second, cases mark certain adverbial functions such as the time, manner, and means of carrying our an action, which are marked by prepositions in English, e.g. by hand, on Friday, with enthusiasm. This function leads to the case system being associated with prepositions.

Remember: in Russian all prepositions are associated with a case which is attached to their objects. Since only nouns can express case, this means that only nouns may be objects of prepositions. AlphaDictionary

Russian noun cases may seem intimidating, but remember: Без труда не вытащишь и рыбку из пруда! (No pain, no gain!)

This page is just a quick overview to Russian cases to help you get your feet wet. See the WikiBooks Entry on Russian Grammatical Cases for a more in-depth exploration of the topic.

Nominative (имени́тельный)

The nominative case is used to indicate the subject of the sentence. The subject is the person, place, or thing that is performing an action or undergoing the state of being. In the sentence Мальчик бежит по дороге. The boy is running along the road., the noun мальчик boy is the subject and occurs in the nominative case. You should also know that all nouns appear in Russian dictionaries in the nominative case; in that sense, it is the "base" case upon which the rest of the declensions are built.

Forming the rest of the noun cases is often simply a matter of adding one or two vowels onto the end of a word in the nominative case, but unfortunately, it's not always that simple!

Accusative (вини́тельный)

Before we start talking about the accusative case, you should understand the concept of direct object.

The term direct object refers to the noun upon which the subject is acting. Take a look at this sentence: Мужчина берёт в руки палку. The man grabs a stick [lit: takes a stick into his hands.] Here the man is the direct object and the stick is the subject of the sentence. As you already know, the noun мужчина man occurs here in the nominative, while the noun палка stick should take the accusative case.

The accusative case is also used with prepositions to describe direction: for example, в руки into his hands, where руки hands appears in the accusative case to indicate the direction of the action taking place.

Dative (да́тельный)

To understand the dative case you should be familiar with the concept of indirect object. The term "indirect object" describes a person, place or thing towards which the action of the verb is being performed. For example, in the sentence Муж принёс жене цветы. The husband brought flowers to his wife., муж husband is the subject and жена wife is the indirect object (i.e., the recipient of the direct object, which in this case is цветы flowers.

What are some other common verbs which "trigger" the dative noun case?

  • дать/давать give
  • позвонить/звонить call
  • сказать/говорить tell, say
  • принести/приносить bring

Instrumental (твори́тельный)

The instrumental case is used to indicate an instrument that helps to perform an action. It might be also used to describe someone's job, or in combination with certain prepositions. The instrumental is frequently used in situations where you would use the prepositions "with" or "by" in English (in the sense of "by means of").

Let's look at some examples. In the sentence Я пишу карандашом. I am writing with a pencil, the noun карандаш pencil is in the instrumental case, because it is the "means" by which the subject of the sentence, я I is writing.

In practice, you'll find that the instrumental case is not always clear-cut, and there are several rules that you must simply remember. Let's consider the sentence Он говорил шёпотом He spoke in a whisper. In this case, шёпот whisper is in the instrumental case because it destribes the manner in which the subject was speaking, rather than the means by which he was speaking.

We also use the instrumental when conveying the idea that something fulfills a certain role or acts as something/someone else. For example, Я работаю преподавателем. I work as a teacher. The noun преподаватель teacher is in the instrumental case because we are trying to convey the idea that the subject fulfills the role of a teacher.

NB! You may be tempted to use the word как how/like/as when trying to translate sentences such as "I work as a waitress." This is a beginner mistake and should be avoided. For example, in the sentence Она работала как официантка., what we're really saying is not that she worked as a waitress, but that she worked like a waitress – i.e., we're comparing the way she works to the work of an actual waitress! The correct way to say this is Она работала официанткой.

Prepositional (предло́жный)

The prepositional case is used to indicate the place or position of an object. It is always used with prepositions. Let's look at some examples:

  • в Москве in Moscow
  • на столе on the table
  • во мне inside of me
  • о фильме about the film

Genitive (роди́тельный)

The genitive case is used in the sentences like Каковы свойства... What are the properties of.... The English equivalent of the genitive case is the preposition "of". The nouns in the genitive answer such questions as Кого? Чего? Of whom? Of what?.

The main role of the genitive is to show where the person or thing belongs to or what it relates to. For example: крыша дома the roof of the house, пачка чая the pack of tea.

The genitive is also used after the words нет, не было, не будет there is not, there was not, there will not be. For example: У него не было денег. He didn't have any money. У меня нет брата. I do not have a brother.
